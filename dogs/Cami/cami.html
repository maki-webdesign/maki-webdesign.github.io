Watch Dog VS Guard Dog

Did you know there is a difference between a watch dog and a guard dog? Although there are many dogs that can perform both duties of being a guard and watch dog, some breeds are specifically bred to “watch” or “guard”.

Watch Dog

A watchdog is one that will use its bark to alert you that a stranger is near your property or approaching your front door. Terriers, are great examples! They are one of a few good family watch dog breeds that barks loudly to alert their owners that a stranger is coming. They are also generally smaller in stature, and have to depend on their owner or a guard dog) to defend its territory.

Guard Dog

A guard dog acts to serve to protect the home and family as well as surrounding areas. A guard dog’s bark is quite the opposite from a watch dogs bark. When a guard dog barks, it means more than a alert, it is a warning that an attack may take place. The best guard dog breeds are generally intimidating in appearance with larger, more muscular bodies, large teeth, and a fearless intensity that easily scares off even the most brazen criminals. Another important guard dog trait is its willingness to bite anyone who is threatening its territory or its humans

Guard Dogs

#1: GERMAN SHEPERD

The German Shepherd is the best guard dog in terms of protection, security, as well as being a great family pet. German Shepherd guard dogs fit well into the family, are affectionate with their people, and protective when necessary.

#2: DOBERMAN PINSCHER

The Doberman Pinscher is a strong and athletic animal that not only gives off a fierce look but they are not afraid to protect their inner circle form danger. This dog was orginally bred to guard.

#3: BELGIAN MALINIOS

The Belgian Malinios is ranked #1 for best guard to own. Malinois are known for being strong and agile enough to provide a guarding and protective role. while also being valued for their natural intelligence.
